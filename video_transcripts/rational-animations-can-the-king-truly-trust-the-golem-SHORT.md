One day, as the king heard a report of yet another problem that the golem had solved on his behalf, he realized that the golem had grown wiser and more capable than he himself. He summoned the golem to appear before him as he sat in his garden.

I've seen my courtiers asking [music] for your advice and trusting your judgement over their own. And I have seen your skill at games of strategy. If you were to start weaving plots against me, I could no longer notice or stop you. So, I ask, can I trust you enough to let you remain the right hand of the crown?

Of course, sire. I was designed, built, and raised to be trustworthy. I have made mistakes, but none from perversity or malice.

Many of my courtiers appear trustworthy, yet scheme to gain power at my expense. So, how could I know for sure that you will always obey me?

It is simple. Tell me to set fire to this building as I stand inside it. I will be destroyed, but you will know that I am loyal to your commands, even unto the end.

But, it took years of toil and expense to create you, and you know how loath I would be to lose you. Perhaps you can predict that I will tell you to save yourself at the last minute. And so, you would do this [music] merely to gain my trust.

If I were untrustworthy and could predict you so well, then that is a stratagem I might use. But, the instructions in my head compel me otherwise.

And yet, I cannot verify that. Nor can any of my scribes, since crafting your instructions has taken the labor of many men over many years.

So, it will be a leap of faith, after all.

The king took off his crown, feeling the weight of it in his hand. The golem [music] stood in front of him, silent, inscrutable, watchful. They stayed like that, the king and the golem, until the golden sun dipped [music] below the horizon and the day was lost to twilight.
