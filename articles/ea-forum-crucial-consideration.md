# Crucial Consideration

*From the EA Forum Topics Wiki*

## Definition

A **crucial consideration** is a consideration with the following two properties:

1. It has a plausible argument that it is correct
2. If it is correct, it would have a significant impact on what we ought to prioritize—potentially radically changing our conclusions about what to do

The term was coined by philosopher Nick Bostrom in the 2014 essay "Crucial Considerations and Wise Philanthropy." A crucial consideration isn't just an important fact—it's a consideration that, if true, would fundamentally transform the overall moral calculus.

## The core idea

Most cause prioritization work involves assembling many considerations and balancing them against each other. A crucial consideration, by contrast, would be so significant that it could dominate all other factors—overturning what had appeared to be well-grounded conclusions.

Examples of things that might qualify as crucial considerations include:

- Evidence that a particular cause area has strong negative unintended consequences that outweigh its direct benefits
- A fundamental realization about what morality requires that changes which beings or outcomes matter
- A discovery that the counterfactual impact of a particular class of interventions is much lower than assumed
- A consideration showing that focusing on the far future matters vastly more (or less) than assumed

## Crucial consideration components

Bostrom also identifies **crucial consideration components**—factors that, in combination with other factors, produce a crucial consideration. These are building blocks that matter significantly in context even if they don't fully override the analysis on their own.

## The deliberation ladder

One way to understand crucial considerations is through the "deliberation ladder." Imagine you are vegan for ethical reasons. You might think: "I'm confident that factory farming causes immense suffering; I'm confident that my diet choices affect demand; therefore, I should be vegan."

But now suppose someone presents you with a crucial consideration: "Actually, the marginal impact of one person's dietary choices on industrial farming is essentially zero because of how the supply chain works." If true, this could change your conclusion—not because your original values were wrong, but because a key factual assumption was overturned.

At a higher level of the deliberation ladder, someone might argue: "Actually, the whole framework of calculating marginal impact on individual products is misguided—what matters is collective action and social norms." This meta-consideration could again reverse the conclusion.

Crucial considerations live at various levels of this ladder.

## Implications for longtermism

Crucial considerations are particularly important in the context of longtermism—the view that the long-term future matters greatly.

On one hand, longtermism itself might be a crucial consideration: if the long-run future contains vast numbers of people (or conscious beings), then the expected value of influencing trajectory changes could dwarf the value of improving things in the near-term.

On the other hand, there might be crucial considerations that *undermine* longtermism—for example, deep uncertainty about whether we can meaningfully influence the far future, or arguments that near-term suffering deserves lexical priority.

Bostrom captures the stakes:

> "Our noblest and most carefully considered attempts to effect change in the world might well be pushing things further away from where they ought to be."

This doesn't mean we should abandon attempts to do good—but it does suggest we should hold our conclusions with appropriate humility and continue searching for crucial considerations we may have missed.

## Why this concept matters for EA

The concept of crucial considerations is important for the EA community because:

1. **It counsels epistemic humility**: Even well-reasoned cause prioritization conclusions could be overturned by considerations we haven't yet discovered.

2. **It justifies investing in research**: If crucial considerations exist that we haven't found yet, there is high value in careful philosophical and empirical work to discover them.

3. **It explains why cause prioritization is hard**: Reasonable people can disagree not just about facts but about which considerations are crucial and how to weight them.

4. **It supports moral uncertainty**: A community aware of crucial considerations will be more open to updating its views based on new arguments and evidence.

The EA community's ongoing discussions about longtermism, animal welfare, and global health partly reflect disagreements about which crucial considerations should drive cause prioritization.
