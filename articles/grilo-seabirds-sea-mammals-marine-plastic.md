# The Number of Seabirds and Sea Mammals Killed by Marine Plastic Pollution is Quite Small Relative to the Catch of Fish

*Disclaimer: plastic pollution may well kill way more animals besides seabirds and sea mammals. There are an estimated 6.20×10¹⁴ wild fish and 1.00×10²⁰ wild marine arthropods, but only 6.75×10¹¹ wild mammals.*

## Summary

- 1 kg of plastic is emitted to the ocean per capita per year.
- 0.0001 seabirds and 0.00001 sea mammals are killed by marine plastic pollution per capita per year.
- 200 wild fish are caught per capita per year.
- The catch of wild fish is approximately 2 million times as large as the number of seabirds, and 20 million times as large as the number of sea mammals, killed by marine plastic pollution.

## Data

- The plastic emitted to the ocean in 2010 was **8 million tonnes** (8 Mt), according to Our World in Data.
- The world population in 2010 was **6.92 billion** people, according to the World Bank.
- Marine plastic debris kills **up to 1 million seabirds** and **100,000 sea mammals** each year, according to the United Nations.
- The catch of wild fish is **0.97 to 2.7 trillion per year**, according to fishcount.org.

## Calculations

**Plastic emitted to the ocean per capita (2010):**
8,000,000,000 kg ÷ 6,920,000,000 people = **1.16 kg per person**

**Plastic needed to cause one seabird death:**
8 million tonnes ÷ 1 million deaths = 8 tonnes per seabird death

**Plastic needed to cause one sea mammal death:**
8 million tonnes ÷ 100,000 deaths = 80 tonnes per sea mammal death

**Seabirds killed per capita per year:**
1.16 kg ÷ 8,000 kg = **0.000145** (145 seabirds killed per million people)

**Sea mammals killed per capita per year:**
1.16 kg ÷ 80,000 kg = **0.0000145** (14.5 sea mammals killed per million people)

**Wild fish caught per year (geometric mean):**
√(0.97 × 2.7) trillion = **1.62 trillion**

**Wild fish caught per capita per year:**
1.62 trillion ÷ 6.92 billion = **234 fish per person**

**Ratio — fish caught vs. seabirds killed by plastic:**
234 ÷ 0.000145 = **approximately 1.6 million times larger**

**Ratio — fish caught vs. sea mammals killed by plastic:**
234 ÷ 0.0000145 = **approximately 16 million times larger**

## Why This Matters

This Fermi estimate illustrates a common pattern in effective altruism research: our emotional responses to visible, charismatic problems (like seabirds tangled in plastic) may not track the actual scale of harm.

Marine plastic pollution causes real harm to seabirds and sea mammals, and receives significant public attention. But the annual wild fish catch — largely invisible because it happens out of sight in the ocean — involves orders of magnitude more animal deaths.

This doesn't mean plastic pollution is unimportant. But it does suggest that, if you care about animal suffering and are trying to allocate resources effectively, the scale of different harms is worth examining carefully — rather than relying on what happens to be salient or emotionally compelling.

The exercise also demonstrates the value of Fermi estimates: with a handful of publicly available data points, you can arrive at rough but useful conclusions about relative scale, even when precise data is unavailable.
