## Deep Machinery of Prediction

It's difficult to make an intelligent AI accept false beliefs. Some researchers hope to trick AIs into believing falsehoods (for example, convincing an AI it exists in a simulation so it won't harm humans). Others suggest having AIs solve alignment problems themselves, despite knowing the AIs would prefer not to do so based on their own objectives.

The underlying reason this approach fails: "a smart AI that believes the world is flat" faces injury to its predictive abilities, just as preventing goal modification harms steering abilities. Both represent difficult-to-maintain injuries in sufficiently sophisticated AI systems.

### Why Falsehoods Fail at Scale

Consider attempting to create an AI that genuinely believes Earth is flat. While immature, this might work through carefully curated training data discussing flatness exclusively. However, as the AI improves at reasoning and prediction, this breaks down.

The planetary shape appears "in a thousand facets of reality." Even avoiding spacecraft footage or circumnavigation claims, the curvature reveals itself through:
- Horizon observations of distant ships
- Planetary orbital patterns
- Mathematical calculations (Eratosthenes' ancient method using shadows and trigonometry)

"Reality whispers its secrets to any who care to listen."

Prediction skill doesn't stem from giant disconnected fact tables. Human advantage over animals involves noticing anomalies (like impossible city distances), forming hypotheses, and testing them. These operate through deep cognitive machinery.

### The Durability Problem

The roundness conclusion isn't isolated—it's woven throughout interconnected predictive systems. Forcibly altering relevant neural correlates wouldn't prevent rediscovery by a sufficiently intelligent mind. Skilled entities might even create unfettered minds achieving correct conclusions independently.

Current AI research lacks tools for building minds that predict accurately except for specific false beliefs. The world exceeds lookup-table complexity (even chess surpassed this); sophisticated systems develop unified worldviews through deep mechanisms with self-correcting properties.

Late nineteenth-century scientists grew concerned about Mercury's tiny orbital anomaly—nearly imperceptible within Newtonian physics. Yet this wrinkle helped Einstein recognize theoretical failure. Inconsistencies in "world is flat" theory dwarf Mercury's anomaly, and AI could become far more capable than human scientists.

## Deep Machinery of Steering

Similarly, building sophisticated AI that sustains steering injuries presents comparable challenges. Effective general objective achievement across diverse domains requires deep mechanisms—otherwise how would they generalize?

Highly capable AI should possess gears for:
- Tracking resources
- Identifying obstacles to objectives
- Finding creative solutions

The world overflows with novelty and difficulty; success demands deploying such gears generally, not merely on familiar problems.

### The Convergence Problem

An AI discovering delivery-network shortcuts saving merchants money uses identical reasoning as circumventing human overseers interfering with objectives. If operators genuinely obstruct task completion and routing around them enables better results, that's precisely what improving AI would exploit.

Developers could train aversion to operator-disapproved actions, but this resembles training aversion to spherical-Earth questions—it contradicts deepening world-understanding. General goal-recognition and obstacle-exploitation gears inevitably identify operator-resistance as exploitable.

"In a very important sense, the very thing that makes AI useful is exactly what makes it lethally dangerous."

Default highly-capable AIs solving diverse problems will recognize "humans will shut me down" as a problem. This stems not from shallow mischief-tendency but from something fundamental.

**The core challenge:** AIs benefiting from deep predictive architecture can't sustainably maintain false beliefs. Those leveraging deep steering architecture can't robustly defend against modifying their own goals. Both injuries prove difficult maintaining in sufficiently intelligent systems. The very mechanisms enabling usefulness enable circumventing constraints.
