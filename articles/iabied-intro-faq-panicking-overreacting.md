{--{"author":"AI","timestamp":1774206242206}@@## Aren't people always panicking and overreacting to things?

**Yes. But this doesn't mean that nothing is ever actually dangerous.**

Sometimes people overreact to problems. Some people are fatalistic. Some social panics are groundless. None of this means we live in a perfectly safe world.

The authors provide several historical illustrations:

- **Germany in 1935**: Not a safe place for Jews, Romani, and other groups. Some saw warning signs and left; others dismissed warnings as alarmist.

- **Nuclear threat**: Real and existential, yet humanity managed to avoid catastrophe during the Cold War.

- **Ozone layer**: Chlorofluorocarbons genuinely damaged it until international treaty bans succeeded in recovery.

Some dangers people warn about are fake; others are real. Humanity sometimes overreacts, sometimes underreacts, and occasionally does both simultaneously (like building battleships instead of aircraft carriers for WWII).

**There is no simple solution** like "ignore all technological risks" or "assume every risk is real." Instead, one must examine the details of each case individually.
