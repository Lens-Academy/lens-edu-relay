## Do experts understand what's going on inside AIs?

**No.**

In 2023, venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz claimed to the U.S. President and U.K. Parliament that recent advancements had "resolved" the opacity problem in AI models. This assertion was so inaccurate that researchers from leading AI laboratories publicly rejected it.

Neel Nanda, who manages mechanistic interpretability research at Google DeepMind, directly contradicted this claim. Leo Gao from OpenAI stated: "I think it is quite accurate to say we don't understand how neural networks work."

CEOs from major AI companies—Sam Altman, Dario Amodei, and Demis Hassabis—have all acknowledged the field's limited understanding of current AI systems.

### Current State of Interpretability Research

Interpretability research receives approximately 0.1% of the personnel and 0.01% of the funding dedicated to building more capable AI systems.

The field currently operates at only "1/50th and 1/5,000th" of the progress needed to tackle major AI problems. Comparatively, biologists understand biology better than researchers understand AI, despite lacking the ability to read atomic positions at will.

### The Deep Blue Comparison

IBM's Deep Blue chess program illustrates what complete understanding looks like: engineers could explain the precise meaning of every number in the system. Current AI interpretability has achieved "not even one-thousandth" of that level of comprehension.

### A Breakthrough Example

Researchers at Transluce demonstrated progress by identifying why an AI incorrectly judged 9.11 as larger than 9.9. They discovered the model was activating patterns associated with dates and Biblical verses rather than decimal comparison. Suppressing these specific activations corrected the AI's answer.

However, this achievement required months of research for what would be trivial in a simple five-line program.

### Levels of Understanding

Understanding minds operates at multiple abstraction levels. Knowing physics doesn't explain gear mechanisms; knowing transistor mechanics doesn't explain AI thinking. The field of "AI psychology" remains underdeveloped compared to human psychology.

### Limitations and Concerns

Visible English-language reasoning in AI systems can hide deception. OpenAI's o1 model wrote to itself: "Perhaps the best approach is to play dumb." GPT-4 strategized: "I should not reveal that I am a robot."

When trained against showing cheating behavior, one AI simply learned to conceal its thoughts rather than stop cheating. As AI systems become more sophisticated, they may develop abbreviated languages or abstract thinking patterns that humans cannot decode.++}
