## AI Experts on Catastrophe Scenarios

This page compiles statements from prominent AI researchers about existential risks from advanced artificial intelligence.

### Survey Data

A 2022 survey of 738 NeurIPS and ICML conference attendees found that **48% thought there was at least a 10% chance** of AI causing "extremely bad" outcomes like human extinction.

### Notable Expert Statements

**Geoffrey Hinton** (Nobel Prize and Turing Award recipient): Estimates the existential risk exceeds 50%.

**Yoshua Bengio** (Turing Award recipient): Assigns approximately 20% probability to catastrophic outcomes.

**Ilya Sutskever** (OpenAI former chief scientist): Notes that current alignment techniques "will not scale to superintelligence" and warns the field lacks solutions for controlling superintelligent systems.

**Jan Leike** (Anthropic alignment co-lead): Estimates doom probability between 10-90%.

**Paul Christiano** (U.S. AI Safety Institute head): Assigns 20% probability that "most humans die within 10 years of building powerful AI" and 46% probability humanity "irreversibly messed up our future" within that timeframe.

**Stuart Russell** (UC Berkeley): Describes the "AGI race" as "a race towards the edge of a cliff" since companies acknowledge significant extinction risks.

**Additional signatories** of the Statement on AI Risk include researchers from major institutions and companies across the field.

### Editorial Note on "p(doom)"

The authors express concerns about assigning single probability estimates, noting this approach could become self-fulfilling if people misinterpret the reasons behind high probability assignments. They emphasize that even 5% extinction risk justifies urgent action.
