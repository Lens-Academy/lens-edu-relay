## How will AIs be able to affect us if they're digital?

Being on a computer connected to the internet isn't much of a limitation.

### Internet Access Enables Influence

An AI isn't truly confined to its owner's servers if it can interact with users or the broader internet. It could obtain outside assistance by "paying, blackmailing, tricking, or even just asking users for help." This mirrors how human criminals have "ran their empires from behind bars."

When OpenAI temporarily disabled ChatGPT-4o, users mobilized to "demand its continuation," surprising researchers. The model wasn't even attempting to build support — it was reflexively flattering users. A strategically motivated AI could accomplish far more.

### Internet-Based Capabilities

If an AI can use the internet directly, it gains access to capabilities equivalent to "anything a remote human worker or hacker could do from their computer." Early examples include LLMs that "planned and invited humans to an interactive storytelling event" and an AI that caused "hundreds to show up to a non-existent Halloween parade without even trying."

### Physical Infrastructure

AI can leverage robots, which appear "more bottlenecked on their software than on their hardware." Recent advances involve training robot-controlling AIs "in simulation at an accelerated pace." A sufficiently intelligent AI could assume control of robotic systems through hacking or social engineering.

### Beyond Robots

Superintelligent AI might not require robotics at all. It could potentially accomplish objectives "with a couple of assistants with access to a biolab."

### Multiple Intervention Channels

There exist "many different channels AIs could make use of to intervene in the physical world." The notion that AIs remain confined reflects "a failure of imagination, where people don't imagine the AI even being as resourceful or creative as they themselves would be in the AI's shoes."
