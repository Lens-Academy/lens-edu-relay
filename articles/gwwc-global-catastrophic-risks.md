# Reducing Global Catastrophic Risks

*By Giving What We Can*

## Overview

Global catastrophic risks—such as pandemics, nuclear conflict, and advanced AI risks—have the potential to impact humanity on an unprecedented scale. Unlike many humanitarian challenges, these risks could permanently derail civilization's long-term trajectory, affecting not just the people alive today but all future generations.

Giving What We Can highlights high-impact organizations working to prevent these threats.

## Why this cause area?

Global catastrophic risks score highly on the key criteria for cause prioritization:

- **Scale**: The potential harm is enormous—potentially affecting billions of people or causing permanent civilizational damage
- **Neglectedness**: Relative to the potential consequences, these risks receive very little funding and attention
- **Tractability**: While hard, there is meaningful work that can be done through policy, research, and institutional capacity-building

Even if one assigns a relatively low probability to catastrophic outcomes, the sheer magnitude of what's at stake—combined with the neglectedness of the field—makes it one of the most compelling cause areas from an expected-value perspective.

## Key risk categories

**Engineered pandemics**: Advances in biotechnology make it increasingly feasible to create pathogens more dangerous than anything that has evolved naturally. Unlike natural pandemics, deliberately engineered threats can be optimized for lethality and transmissibility.

**Nuclear conflict**: Despite the end of the Cold War, thousands of nuclear weapons remain on high alert. The risk of miscalculation, accident, or escalation remains real. A large-scale nuclear exchange could cause a "nuclear winter" affecting global agriculture.

**Advanced AI**: The development of highly capable AI systems poses risks both from misalignment (AI pursuing goals that diverge from human values) and misuse (AI enabling bad actors to cause unprecedented harm). Managing this transition safely is a major challenge.

**Other risks**: Researchers also track risks from extreme climate change, asteroid impacts, and other scenarios that could cause civilizational damage.

## High-impact organizations

Giving What We Can highlights organizations working on these risks as potentially high-impact giving opportunities. These include organizations focused on biosecurity research and policy, nuclear risk reduction, AI safety research, and broader existential risk analysis and advocacy.
