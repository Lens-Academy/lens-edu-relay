## The Shallowness of Current AIs

The page documents specific examples demonstrating gaps in current AI capabilities:

### Game Playing Issues

Claude 3.7 Sonnet became trapped in repetitive patterns while attempting a simple Pokemon game. KataGo, a leading Go AI, possessed exploitable blind spots that even amateur players wouldn't fall for.

### Visual Recognition Gaps

Modern multimodal language models struggle with tasks that "most human 4th graders can handle," including interpreting analog clocks and calendars.

### Logic Problems

Current LLMs notoriously fail variations of classic riddles, unable to resist providing trick answers despite straightforward solutions.

### Balancing AI Capabilities

The resource acknowledges that modern AIs achieve notable feats—winning gold medal standards in mathematical olympiads and exceeding human performance in numerous domains. However, their skillset remains fundamentally "strange" relative to human strengths and weaknesses.

The authors emphasize that this shallowness "is of sorts" to contemporary systems, but progress continues steadily. The critical question remaining concerns how long such limitations persist before they're overcome.

Human capability gaps don't necessarily predict AI trajectory, given that "AIs radically and fundamentally differ from humans in many ways."
