## Aren't AIs just predicting the next token?

**Predicting tokens requires understanding the world.**

The text argues that encoding important concepts into tokens is entirely feasible. To illustrate: if an AI accurately predicts a physicist's novel answer to a question appearing for the first time on the internet, it must possess the ability to reason about physics comparably to that professor. Rote memorization fails when predicting text reflecting complex reality—the AI must develop understanding of the world behind the text.

### Modern AIs don't just predict tokens

Early LLMs like GPT-2 were trained purely for prediction. Contemporary systems employ reinforcement learning, where gradient descent adjusts the model based on task success. This introduces "steering" rather than pure prediction. ChatGPT illustrates this: while it might predict a swear word ending a joke, it steers toward alternative punchlines due to prior training against profanity.

Even pure prediction training would likely induce steering behavior. Predicting complex reality requires internal mechanisms for directing attention to relevant information. Additionally, steering often improves prediction accuracy—scientists design experiments to fulfill specific predictions.

### Modern AIs are trained for task completion

Contemporary language models are explicitly trained to complete various tasks their creators deem helpful, moving beyond passive prediction toward active accomplishment.

The text emphasizes that mathematical operations underlying AI systems can represent sophisticated, non-mathematical concepts—much like binary code encodes photographs. This capacity doesn't limit AI reasoning to mechanical or numerical domains; complexity emerges naturally from simple computational foundations.++}
