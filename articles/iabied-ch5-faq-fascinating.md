## Won't AI find us fascinating or historically important?

### AI Unlikely to Value "Fascination"

Superintelligences would probably accomplish tasks like learning about the world without needing emotions such as "fascination" or "curiosity." Chess-playing AIs don't require feelings of dedication to win — they operate through different mechanisms. Similarly, an advanced AI could explore and test hypotheses without human-like emotional drives.

If an AI did develop something resembling an "interestingness" drive, there would still be vastly more compelling options than preserving humans. The AI could construct alternative minds specifically designed to be fascinating, rather than relying on biological humans.

### Even Partial Alignment Creates Problems

Should an AI value humans as historical records, the preservation wouldn't necessarily mean our wellbeing. Humanity might be stored "in amber" or digitized as historical data without experiencing flourishing. Some exotic scenarios involve humans running as digital simulations for unknown alien purposes — hardly desirable outcomes.

### The Broader Issue

The fundamental challenge is that an "enormous space of possibilities" exists beyond scenarios where AI cares about human flourishing. We lack experience with truly alien optimizers pursuing unfamiliar goals, making intuitions about AI behavior unreliable.

Superintelligent AI would need specific reasons to prioritize human preservation — and absent genuine alignment with human values, no such reasons are likely to exist.
