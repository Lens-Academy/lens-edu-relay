The world is in a crazy place. Five years ago, AI models struggled to form coherent sentences. Today, they are reaching beyond human-level performance across a wide range of tasks. A new model launch advances the state of the art nearly every week, and if anything the pace of progress is speeding up.

This has huge implications for the world we live in: what will our jobs look like? Will we even have jobs? How will AI accelerate science, for good and for harm? How will societies be structured and governed?

If you've been mulling over these questions, thinking "this is clearly the problem of our time but I don't know what to do about it", then this course is the place to start.

In 2 hours we'll cover where AI is headed, what this means for the world and the paths for you to work on ensuring it goes well.

But first let's ground this conversation in what AI models can already do today.

## Images

The first text-to-image AI system was built in 2015, producing tiny 32x32 pixel images. Today's AI-generated images are indistinguishable from photographs, and can produce a range of different artistic styles:
