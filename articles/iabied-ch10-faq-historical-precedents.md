# Won't AI differ from all the historical precedents?

**Yes.**

Some unique features of the AI alignment challenge will make it easier than engineering a nuclear power plant, while others will make it harder. Overall, nuclear weapons and plants seem "dramatically simpler to manage than smarter-than-human AI."

The industry suggests AI itself could help with alignment, but this requires the AI to already be "very capable, and very aligned" to assist effectively. Humans could theoretically control AI cognitive dynamics if they understood them perfectly, but "nobody is anywhere near that level of understanding in real life."

AI presents harder challenges than past problems:

1. Nuclear weapons aren't smarter than humans
2. Nuclear weapons don't self-replicate or self-improve
3. Nuclear war wouldn't necessarily eliminate all humanity
4. Companies aren't scaling nuclear stockpiles exponentially yearly
5. Nuclear weapon science is well-understood and predictable
6. Nuclear weapons don't make independent plans
7. There's global consensus that nuclear war is unacceptable
8. Nuclear weapons are difficult to replicate or mass-produce
9. World powers treat nuclear war as genuinely unacceptable

Critically, "humanity only gets one shot at getting superintelligence right." Unlike nuclear plants where failures teach lessons, superintelligence failure means extinction with no opportunity to learn and improve.

"There is no established playbook for ASI."
