**Solving difficult challenges requires AIs to take more and more initiative.**

Consider an AI tasked with curing Alzheimer's disease. It would need persistence when facing obstacles, strategic thinking about where to focus attention, and the ability to determine what knowledge it needs and conduct its own experiments. While simpler diseases might require only straightforward drug discoveries, curing conditions like cancer or aging would likely require deeply agentic AI systems—dogged, strategic, and goal-oriented enough to develop sophisticated biochemical understanding.

The AI industry continuously pushes systems to become more skilled at solving increasingly important problems. This naturally drives AIs toward greater driven behavior, an effect already visible in systems like OpenAI's o1. **Being tenacious is helpful even when the target is not quite right.**

Prehistoric humans who actively pursued specific goals—better meals, sharper tools, social status, attractive mates—were more evolutionarily successful than passive individuals. Natural selection "grew" humans with numerous distinct drives they persistently pursue.

The specific way humans desire things may be coincidental, but doggedly pursuing objectives appears essential for accomplishing difficult goals. While individual humans vary in tenacity, humanity's macro-level scientific and engineering achievements depend on driven individuals and institutions.

Evolution could theoretically have created a single overriding drive for reproduction, with other goals pursued solely for that purpose. Instead, it created separate desires for food, tools, and other ends. Having distinct drives and purposes is useful—so useful it helps accomplish tasks even when desires don't precisely match the objective.

However, this benefit has limits. Once entities become sufficiently intelligent, their behavior can sharply diverge from their "training" target. Millions of years of evolution produced humanity, which promptly invented birth control.

**Being grown rather than crafted, AIs are liable to wind up with the wrong targets.**
