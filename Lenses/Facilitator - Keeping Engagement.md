#### Text
content::
Long sessions tend to decay. The energy you have at minute 10 is not the energy you have at minute 40. Some practical antidotes:

- **Vary the format.** Whole-group → pair → whole-group → individual reflection → whole-group. The shifts reset attention.
- **Signpost what's next.** *"We'll spend 10 minutes on this, then move to the second question."* People engage harder when they know the shape.
- **Use specific names.** *"Many of you mentioned X"* is dead air. *"Maya raised X; Tariq pushed back; Aanya took a third angle"* is alive.
- **Surface what hasn't been said.** Halfway through, ask: *"What hasn't anyone said yet that should be said?"* — this can surface contributions people were holding back, and gives the cohort a way to redirect if the conversation has drifted from what they actually wanted to discuss.
