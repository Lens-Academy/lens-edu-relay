\## Four antidotes--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165939082}@@Long sessions tend to decay. The energy you have at minute 10 is not the energy you have at minute 40. Some practical antidotes:++}

- **Vary the format.** Whole-group → pair → whole-group → individual reflection → whole-group. The shifts reset attention.
- **Signpost what's next.** *"We'll spend 10 minutes on this, then move to the second question."* People engage harder when they know the shape.
- **Use specific names.** *"Many of you mentioned X"* is dead air. *"Maya raised X; Tariq pushed back; Aanya took a third angle"* is alive.
- **Surface what hasn't been said.** Halfway through, ask: *"What hasn't anyone said yet that should be said?"* — this {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165939082}@@often produces the best--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165939082}@@can surface++} contributions {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165939082}@@of the session.
