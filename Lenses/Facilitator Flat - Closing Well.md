\## Three-part close

1. **Reflect** — *"What's the one thing each of you is taking away?"* (One sentence per person; don't let it sprawl.)
2. **Integrate** — *"What might you do with this between now and next session?"* (Action-oriented, not abstract.)
3. **Ritual** — A small consistent move that signals the session is done. A shared phrase, a closing breath, a single-word check-out. Doesn't have to be elaborate; has to be consistent across sessions.

\## ++}Closing a cohort (not just a session)

If you're facilitating an arc of {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@4-8--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@4–8++} sessions across weeks, the final session needs more weight than the others. Three things to add:

- **Reflect across the arc, not just the last session.** *"What's different in how you think about this material now, vs. when we started?"*
- **Name the cohort.** What was this group specifically like? What was the texture? Concrete observations land more than generic gratitude.
- **Set up the after-life.** What happens after the formal sessions end? A Slack channel that stays open? A future check-in? An invitation to reconnect in 3 months?{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@ The strongest cohorts have a life beyond the syllabus.++}

\## Building a cohort, not just a class

{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@Across --}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@A small operational reframe: across ++}the arc of sessions, treat the cohort as something you're *building* (a peer network) rather than just *teaching* (a class). The {++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@two are not the same — and the ++}small moves that make a {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@difference:--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@difference are:++}

- **Names and 1 fact about each person, used early and often.** *"Aanya, you mentioned working in healthcare AI — does this connect?"*
- **Connect participants to each other, not just to you.** *"Tariq, what you're saying sounds like what Maya raised in Week 2 — have you two talked?"*
- **Off-session connection.** A Slack channel, a shared doc, a between-session optional check-in — anything that makes the cohort exist outside the 60 minutes you're together.
- **Acknowledge transitions.** First session, halfway, final session — these are different, and treating them differently helps the group feel the arc.

By Week 6, a well-tended cohort knows each other's names, references each other's points, and continues talking after you've left the call. That's the real success metric. Knowledge transfer was a bonus.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781127027407}@@
