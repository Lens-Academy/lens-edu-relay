When a session goes hard — someone pushes back on you, a topic gets heated, the room goes silent in a bad way — you may tend to default to one of two failure modes. Knowing which one is yours, before it happens, makes it half as bad when it does.

\## The pufferfish and the hermit crab

This is from *The Authentic Facilitator* (Sara Ness) and it's the most useful self-knowledge frame in this entire course.

**The pufferfish** puffs up under pressure. They get bigger, more assertive, more in-charge. They take over the room. They might raise their voice, or get more directive, or start explaining things instead of asking questions. They overcorrect into control.

**The hermit crab** retreats under pressure. They get smaller, more deferential, less willing to push or redirect. They let the dominant voice keep dominating. They hide. They overcorrect into accommodation.

Most people have a clear default. Yours probably already came to mind reading those descriptions.
