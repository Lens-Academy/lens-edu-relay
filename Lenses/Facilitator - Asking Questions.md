\## Watch

**Video — Questioning Techniques for Teachers — Jonathan Sandling (10 min):** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CaE_4NlNM74

\## Three frameworks

\### PPPB — Pose, Pause, Pounce, Bounce

1. **Pose** the question to the whole group (not to one person — that lets the others off the hook).
2. **Pause.** Silently. Count to seven. (It feels much longer than it is.)
3. **Pounce** — call on a specific person. The pause means everyone prepared an answer, not just whoever speaks first.
4. **Bounce** — take their answer and bounce it to someone else: *"Maya, what do you make of that?"*

\### ABC — Agree, Build, Challenge

After someone answers, give the next person a structured response choice:
- **Agree** with the previous answer and add something.
- **Build** on it — extend, refine, develop.
- **Challenge** it — disagree with a specific part.

Especially powerful because it gives shy participants a scaffolded way to speak. They're not generating a new answer from scratch; they're responding to one that's already in the room.

\### EEAA — Engage, Evaluate, Apply, Adapt

When a participant gives a substantive answer, your follow-up should be one of:
- **Engage** — *"Say more about that."*
- **Evaluate** — *"What's the evidence?"*
- **Apply** — *"Where would that show up in practice?"*
- **Adapt** — *"How would your answer change if X?"*

Most rookie facilitators default to "Engage" for everything. Variety matters.

\## Five workhorse question patterns

- **Pre-mortem:** "Imagine this idea fails in five years. What was the reason?"
- **Translate:** "How would you explain this to someone outside this field?"
- **Disagreement:** "What's the strongest objection to what you just said?"
- **Instance:** "Can you give a concrete example?"
- **Threshold:** "What would have to be true for you to change your mind?"

\## After

Take one of your next session's planned questions. Write out:

1. **PPPB version:** how would you pose it, the pause length, who you'd pounce on first?
2. **A possible answer a participant might give** — and which EEAA move you'd use as a follow-up.

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just watched the Sandling video.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Describe PPPB, ABC, EEAA frameworks. Apply PPPB to one prepared question and name EEAA follow-up.

Key concepts:
- PPPB: Pose → Pause (count to 7) → Pounce → Bounce
- ABC: Agree / Build / Challenge — gives shy participants a scaffold
- EEAA: Engage, Evaluate, Apply, Adapt — variety beats default "engage"
- Five workhorse patterns: pre-mortem, translate, disagreement, instance, threshold

Discussion guidance:
- If they skip the pause ("7 seconds is awkward"), push: silence is the most under-used facilitator tool. Practice it in low-stakes situations first.
- If they always reach for "Engage" — that's the rookie pattern. Variety is the skill.
- If they don't have a prepared question, ask them to invent one on the spot for a hypothetical session.

Probe: "Of the five workhorse patterns, which one are you most reluctant to use? Why?"
