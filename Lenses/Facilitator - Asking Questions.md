**Three frameworks** worth knowing, all covered in the video:++}

\### PPPB — Pose, Pause, Pounce, Bounce

1. **Pose** the question to the whole group (not to one person — that lets the others off the hook).
2. **Pause.** Silently. Count to seven. (It feels much longer than it is.)
3. **Pounce** — call on a specific person. The pause means everyone prepared an answer, not just whoever speaks first.
4. **Bounce** — take their answer and bounce it to someone else: *"Maya, what do you make of that?"*

\### ABC — Agree, Build, Challenge

After someone answers, {++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165768768}@@you could ++}give the next person a structured response choice:
- **Agree** with the previous answer and add something.
- **Build** on it — extend, refine, develop.
- **Challenge** it — disagree with a specific part.

{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165768768}@@Especially powerful because it--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165768768}@@This++} gives shy participants a scaffolded way to speak. They're not generating a new answer from scratch; they're responding to one that's already in the room.

\### EEAA — Engage, Evaluate, Apply, Adapt

When a participant gives a substantive answer, your follow-up {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165768768}@@should--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781165768768}@@could++} be one of:
- **Engage** — *"Say more about that."*
- **Evaluate** — *"What's the evidence?"*
- **Apply** — *"Where would that show up in practice?"*
- **Adapt** — *"How would your answer change if X?"*
