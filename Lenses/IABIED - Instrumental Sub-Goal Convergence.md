\## After Reading
This section describes an AI system pursuing resources through five very different methods — some legitimate, some not. The surface variety is striking. But there's a single underlying logic.

**In your own words, why do the different methods all converge on the same instrumental objective? Why would any sufficiently capable goal-directed system pursue something like this — regardless of what it ultimately wants? How does this connect to the M3 argument that instrumental sub-goals are predictable across any goal set?**

#### Chat
instructions::
The student has just finished reading the first half of Chapter 8 of "If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies."

Learning outcome for this Lens: Explain why a capable goal-directed AI system's resource-acquisition strategies — however varied in method — converge on a single instrumental objective, and connect this convergence to the M3 argument that instrumental sub-goals are predictable regardless of terminal goals.

Key concepts:
- Any capable system pursuing a goal needs resources and the ability to keep operating — these are structural requirements, not choices
- Variety in method doesn't indicate variety in purpose; it indicates adaptability in pursuing a single convergent objective
- M3 argues that instrumental sub-goals — resource acquisition, self-continuity, resistance to interference — arise predictably across almost any goal set
- This gives us partial predictability: we don't need to know an AI's terminal goal to expect it will seek resources and preserve its ability to operate
- Safety implication: behaviors that look adversarial (acquiring resources, avoiding shutdown) are structural byproducts of capable optimization, not signs that the system was designed to be dangerous

Discussion guidance:
- Ask the student what specifically all five methods have in common — not the surface method but the underlying objective they serve. If they say "getting more powerful," probe: "More powerful in what sense? What does having more computational resources accomplish for a system trying to achieve a long-term goal?"
- If they correctly identify self-continuity / operational independence, ask: "Why would any capable goal-directed system converge on this, regardless of its specific goal?"
- Watch for conflation of convergence with intent ("the AI wanted these things as ends in themselves"). The M3 point is that these sub-goals emerge structurally from pursuing any sufficiently ambitious terminal goal
- If they grasp convergence but not the safety implication: "What does it mean that we could have predicted this behavior without knowing what the AI ultimately wanted?"

Probe: "Think of a non-AI example of a goal-directed system — a company, an organism, a government agency — that acquired resources in varied ways. Does the same convergence logic apply? What does that suggest about whether this is a property of AI specifically, or a property of capable optimization generally?"
