#### Text
content::
\## Read

\### Prep that actually moves session quality (target: 20-30 minutes)

1. **Did the reading yourself, once, with the participant's eye.** What was confusing? What was unexpected? What did you want to argue with? Those moments are where the session will live.

2. **Three good questions, ordered.** One opener (low-stakes, gets people talking), one mid-session deepener (the heart of the discussion), one applicator (what does this mean for what we actually do?). More than three is excess prep; you won't use them.

3. **A backup plan for one foreseeable problem.** Usually one of: nobody did the reading; one person dominates; the topic is touchier than expected. Pick the most likely and have a first move ready.

4. **The session shape.** Roughly how many minutes for opening, main discussion, closing. Even a loose timing keeps you from running over the heart of the session.

\### Prep that's busywork

- **Scripting your opening word-for-word.** It will sound canned. You'll either follow the script (stiff) or abandon it (waste).
- **Writing out detailed answers to your own questions.** You shouldn't be answering them.
- **Designing elaborate activities for a 45-minute session.** The activity is the discussion.
- **Re-reading the reading 4 times.** Diminishing returns hit after the second pass.

\## After

For your next session: write down your three questions (opener / deepener / applicator) and the one backup plan. Set a 30-minute timer for everything else. When the timer goes, stop.

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just read the prep-discipline material.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Distinguish prep that moves session quality from prep that is busywork. Build a 20-30 minute prep template for their next session.

Key concepts:
- High-value prep ceiling: ~20-30 min
- Four moves that matter: read with participant's eye; three questions ordered (opener/deepener/applicator); one backup plan; rough session shape
- Busywork: scripting, writing answers to your own questions, elaborate activities, re-reading 4x
- The "three questions ordered" is specifically diverse types, not three on one theme

Discussion guidance:
- If they want to do "more thorough prep," push: thorough toward what end? Most over-prep is anxiety management for the facilitator, not session quality.
- If they want to script the opening, walk them through what happens: stiff if followed, wasted if abandoned. Better to have one strong opening question and trust yourself with the framing in real time.

Probe: "What's the prep move you're most reluctant to give up — and what would have to be true for you to drop it?"
