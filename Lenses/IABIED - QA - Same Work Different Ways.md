#### Text
content::
This article argues that when you only know one way something works (like biological brains), you tend to wrongly assume all implementations must share those characteristics. Just as planes fly without flapping wings and transistors switch 800 million times faster than neurons, AI systems can achieve intelligence through radically different mechanisms than biological brains use.
