#### Text
content:: Module 2 — *How People Learn*. **Time: ~35 minutes. Format: 2 readings + 1 short framework + 1 reflection exercise.**

Facilitation works only if discussion produces learning. Most people assume it does. The evidence is more specific than that.

This module gives you the empirical case behind the format. You'll need it for two reasons: so you can explain to participants — when they ask — *why* we don't just summarise the reading at the top; and so when a session feels slow or chaotic, you can tell whether it's working despite the feeling, or actually failing.

By the end you should be able to: name at least three cognitive-science mechanisms that justify a discussion format; describe the flipped-classroom approach in one sentence; and structure a question using Think-Pair-Share.
