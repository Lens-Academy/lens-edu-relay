The key insight: **norms imposed from outside don't get followed. Norms agreed to in conversation do.** A facilitator who reads out a list of rules has made the cohort no more likely to follow them than if they'd said nothing.

\## Five candidate norms worth considering

These are starting points, not gospel. Adapt them with your group:

1. **Steel-man before you push back.** Before you disagree, restate the other view in a way the other person would recognise.
2. **Share airtime.** If you've spoken twice in a row, hold back until others have spoken once. Quiet members: please push yourselves to speak by Week 3.
3. **Confidentiality on identity, not on ideas.** What was said stays here; what was thought, take with you.
4. **It's OK to change your mind in the room.** Saying "I've updated since you said that" is a high-status move here, not a low-status one.
5. **One conversation at a time.** No side chats in Zoom DMs, no muted-but-side-talking. If something needs saying, say it to the group.

\## The setup move

In Week 1, don't read the list out. Instead:

> "Before we get into the material — what would each of you need from this group to make this worth your time? What would be a deal-breaker?"

Collect 6-10 answers on a shared doc or whiteboard. Then — *and only then* — propose a few of your own additions, framed as questions: *"What about X — anyone want that in the list?"*

The list that ends up on the doc, with the cohort's own language, is the one they'll actually use.

\## After

For your first session: which of the five candidate norms would you most want to be in the cohort's list? If they don't surface it themselves, how will you propose it without imposing?

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just consumed the norm-setting material.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Explain why norms imposed get less follow-through than norms co-created, name 3-5 candidate norms, and describe the Week-1 setup move.

Key concepts:
- Imposed norms don't get internalised; co-created norms do
- 5 candidate norms: steel-man before push back; share airtime; confidentiality on identity not ideas; changing your mind is high-status; one conversation at a time
- Week-1 setup: ask cohort first what they need; then add your own as questions

Discussion guidance:
- If they're tempted to "save time" by reading the list out, push: what's the cost of saving 10 minutes? (The norms don't stick.)
- If they want to skip norm-setting entirely ("the cohort will figure it out"), push: the cohort will figure out norms; you just won't get to influence which ones.

Probe: "Of the five candidate norms, which one would you have the hardest time enforcing in the moment when a participant violates it? Why?"
