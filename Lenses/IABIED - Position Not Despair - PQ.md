#### Text
content:: When experts say a project should not be attempted, you can hear two very different things in that sentence:

- *"Nothing can be done."* — the situation is hopeless; the right response is to give up.
- *"This specific attempt, with this specific level of understanding, should not be attempted."* — the situation calls for a different response than the one being offered.

These sound similar but they imply opposite responses. **Before you read the rest of Chapter 10, think of a real-world example you know — a building that wasn't built, an experiment that wasn't run, a launch that was scrubbed — where someone made the second statement and was right.**

What was the difference between *that* call and despair? What did the right call point toward instead?
