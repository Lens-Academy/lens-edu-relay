#### Text
content:: Before reading the Introduction: What kinds of things about the future do you feel fairly confident predicting? What makes a prediction feel like a "sure thing" versus a "coin flip"? Take a moment to jot down your answer before reading. There's no right or wrong answers to priming questions, we just want you to gather your thoughts before you're exposed to the ideas in the book. The authors have a specific framework for this.

#### Chat
instructions::
The student is about to read the Introduction to "If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies."

They just answered: "What kinds of things about the future do you feel confident predicting? What makes a prediction feel like a sure thing versus a coin flip?"

Acknowledge their answer in 1–2 sentences. Do not explain or preview the hard/easy calls framework — they will encounter it in the reading. Close with something like: "Keep that in mind as you read. Head to the Introduction when you're ready and come back when you've finished."
