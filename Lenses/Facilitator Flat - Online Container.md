\## What's harder online

- Reading body language and group energy
- Silence (it feels worse than it is)
- Side connections during breaks
- Latecomers slipping in unnoticed

\## What's easier online

- Sharing reference material live (chat, screenshare)
- Pair work (breakout rooms are frictionless)
- Recording (if consented)
- Including people across timezones who couldn't otherwise be in the room

A good online facilitator leans into the second list rather than mourning the first.

\## After

Pick one item from the "harder" list. What's a workaround you'd try? Pick one item from the "easier" list. How will you actively use that affordance in your next session?

- Online is its own medium, not degraded in-person

- Easier: live reference material, breakout rooms, recording, timezone inclusion
- The skill is leaning into the second list

Discussion guidance:
- If they mourn the harder things ("if only we could meet in person") — redirect. The format is online. What does this format make possible?
- Push on the breakout-room affordance. Most rookies underuse it.

Probe: "What's one online-specific move you've seen done well in a session you were a participant in — and what made it work?"
