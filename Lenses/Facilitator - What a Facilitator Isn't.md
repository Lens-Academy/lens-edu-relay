\## Read

--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@ ++}**You're not a teacher.** You don't have to know more than your participants. Several of them may know more than you. Your job isn't expertise; it's a process.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@

--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@
- ++}**Make room for emotions, but {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@you’re--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@you're++} not a therapist** — AI safety is unusually emotional material, and you can't pretend otherwise. Participants will sometimes arrive heavy: a reading has shaken them, a recent news cycle landed hard, they're rethinking their career. Your job isn't to do therapy, but please do make space for the emotion — acknowledge it, let it sit, and let the cohort hold it together when that's appropriate. If something is too big for the session, signpost an off-ramp ("happy to talk after, or I can connect you with X"). The line: feel free to {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@*name*--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@name++} and {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@*witness*--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@witness++} emotion; don't try to {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@*resolve*--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@resolve++} it.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@

--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@
- ++}**You're not (just) a host.** Being warm and welcoming matters — cohorts open up faster with a facilitator they like. But likability isn't the goal; it's a side effect of doing the job well. A facilitator who optimises for being liked stops pushing on weak claims, because pushing risks the warmth. The aim is both: a facilitator the cohort trusts because they're warm and because they don't let things slide.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@

--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@
- ++}**You're not responsible for every outcome.** If a participant doesn't engage, despite your good moves, that's partly on them. Care, but don't internalise.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@

--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@
- ++}**You're not their boss.** You can ask, request, and invite. You can't compel. The mode is "with," not "above."

{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@\## --}What you {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@ARE--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@**are**++} responsible {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@for--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@for:++}

- That the space is safe enough for honest contribution.
- That the time is used in a way that serves the group's learning.
- That weak claims don't pass unexamined just because they're popular.
- That every voice gets enough air, and no one voice takes too much.
- That the session has a clear shape — a beginning, a middle, and an end.{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781166761290}@@

\## After

For each of the five "not"s — pick the one you are most likely to slip into. (Most people know within seconds.) Note it. The failure mode that comes from taking on something that isn't yours is real: trying to be the teacher makes the room passive; trying to be the therapist makes everyone uncomfortable; optimising to be liked makes you stop facilitating.

instructions::
The trainee has just read the boundaries-of-the-role section.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Identify at least three roles a facilitator is NOT responsible for, and articulate what they ARE responsible for in concrete terms.

Key concepts:
- The five "not"s: teacher, therapist, host, responsible for every outcome, their boss
- The five "are"s: safety, time, examined claims, voice distribution, session shape
- Each "not" has a specific failure mode if you take it on anyway
- "With," not "above" — facilitators ask, request, invite; they don't compel

Discussion guidance:
- Push on "not a host." Most rookies optimise for being liked. Ask: "What would change about your sessions if you stopped trying to be liked?"
- Push on "not a therapist." If they say "but what if someone shares something real?" — walk through the signposting move (acknowledge briefly, offer off-ramp, return to material).
- If they conflate "I'm not responsible for every outcome" with "I don't care" — clarify the distinction. Care without internalising.

Probe: "Of the five things you ARE responsible for, which one is the easiest to drop when a session is going well? That's the one to watch."--}
