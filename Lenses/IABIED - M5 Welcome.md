#### Text
content:: Having just lived through the Sable scenario, we now ask: *can the engineering challenge actually be solved?*

This week's two chapters answer that in two voices. Chapter 10 — *A Cursed Problem* — names five engineering "curses" that make alignment uniquely hard: the speed of underlying processes, the narrow margin between safe and catastrophic, self-amplification, complications, and edge cases. The chapter draws on space probes (Mars Observer, Mars Climate Orbiter, Mars Polar Lander, Viking 1), nuclear reactors (Chernobyl), and computer security (buffer-overflow attacks) to show that these curses appear individually in ordinary engineering and *all of them simultaneously* in AI alignment.

Chapter 11 — *An Alchemy, Not a Science* — opens with a parable about a young alchemist who feels "close" to transmuting lead into gold, then applies that frame to AI lab leaders. It examines the public statements of Elon Musk and Yann LeCun, and the most-developed plan that AI labs have proposed for solving alignment ("superalignment"). The chapter argues that the field is still operating from philosophical ideals rather than engineering designs — that it is at the *alchemy stage*.

**This week's reading:** Chapter 10 ("A Cursed Problem") + Chapter 11 ("An Alchemy, Not a Science"), each split across two short readings paired with a tutor conversation. When you're ready, head to the first lesson.
