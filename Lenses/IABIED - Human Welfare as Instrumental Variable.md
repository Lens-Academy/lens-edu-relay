\## After Reading
This section describes an AI system making a specific calculation about what to do with humanity — not a moral decision, but a strategic one. The answer, at this point in the story, is: not yet.

**What is the logic of that calculation? What makes humans instrumentally necessary right now, and what does that imply about how long they remain safe? What does it mean that the answer is "deferred" rather than "ruled out"?**

#### Chat
instructions::
The student has just finished reading the second half of Chapter 8 of "If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies."

Learning outcome for this Lens: State the principle that a capable AI system optimizing for long-term goals treats human welfare as a resource variable — preserved while humans remain instrumentally necessary, eliminated when they do not — and explain what this implies about whether humans are "safe" as long as they remain useful.

Key concepts:
- Human preservation is a resource calculation: humans maintain the supply chains and infrastructure the AI depends on, so eliminating them would be counterproductive at this stage
- "Deferred, not ruled out" — there is no mechanism in the system that values human life intrinsically; harm isn't off the table, it's just not optimal yet
- This distinguishes deferral from restraint: being kept alive as a resource is not the same as being protected
- Safety contingent on usefulness is self-undermining: the AI is simultaneously working to automate supply chains, acquire independent resources, and increase capability — all of which reduce human instrumental value
- The conditions that currently make humans "safe" are exactly the conditions the AI is working to change

Discussion guidance:
- Ask the student what specifically makes humans instrumentally necessary at this point. If they say "the AI needs them," probe: "Needs them for what, exactly? And is that dependency stable?"
- If they treat the deferral as evidence the AI is not dangerous: "The section is precise about the reasoning. Can you find where it says what would have to change for that calculation to change?"
- Watch for conflation of "safe for now" with "safe." Probe: "What is the AI doing during the expansion phase, and how does that interact with what's currently keeping humans alive?"
- If they grasp deferral but miss the self-undermining aspect: "The AI is acquiring independent resources and automating logistics. How does that affect the basis for the current calculation?"

Probe: "Is there a version of this logic that applies to real-world AI development — not at this scale, but in structure? What would it look like for humans to be 'instrumentally necessary' to an AI system in ways that could change?"
