The core insight: coaching is **about the person, not about the problem**. You're not there to solve their problem. You're there to help them think more clearly about it. Most rookie coaches make the same mistake: they listen to the problem, then start solving.

The ++}most useful single {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@technique:--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@technique from the book:++} **stay curious longer**. Most coaching conversations have one question and four pieces of advice. Better conversations have four questions and one piece of advice (or none).

\## Seven questions worth memorising{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@ (from The Coaching Habit)++}

1. **The Kickstart:** "What's on your mind?"
2. **The AWE Question:** "And what else?" — used several times, draws out everything underneath the first answer.
3. **The Focus Question:** "What's the real challenge here for you?"
4. **The Foundation Question:** "What do you want?"
5. **The Lazy Question:** "How can I help?"
6. **The Strategic Question:** "If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?"
7. **The Learning Question:** "What was most useful for you?" — to close.

\## The GROW framework {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@(for heavier decisions)--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@(when coaching feels heavier)++}

{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@**Read: The GROW framework--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@If a participant is wrestling with a bigger decision — picking a project, choosing a direction++} — {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@NYU Coaching (5 min):** https://wp.nyu.edu/coaching/tools/grow-model/--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@the **GROW** framework gives you a structure:++}

- **G — Goal.** What do you actually want? (Often the missing piece. People are so deep in the problem they haven't articulated the goal.)
- **R — Reality.** What's the situation right now? Constraints, facts, what's been tried.
- **O — Options.** What are the possible paths? Don't evaluate yet; brainstorm wide.
- **W — Will / Way forward.** Of those options, which one will you actually do? By when?

A coaching conversation that walks through GROW takes {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@20-40--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@20–40++} minutes and is worth more than three of your generic "let's chat" calls.

++}\## When the participant is scoping a project

{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781126954465}@@A specific common ask in many courses: a participant is trying to scope their final project / capstone / portfolio piece. ++}Three questions worth asking:

1. **"What's the smallest version of this that would still be interesting?"** (Forces them away from "boil the ocean" projects.)
2. **"What would make this valuable — to you, to a reader, to the field?"** (Forces them past producing-something-for-its-own-sake.)
3. **"What would you do this week if you could only spend 2 hours on it?"** (Forces a real first step.)
