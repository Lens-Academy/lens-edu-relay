content::
This article argues that the history of science repeatedly shows "mysterious" phenomena -- from the animation of bodies to the creation of art -- turning out to be built from mundane, understandable parts. The vitalists were wrong about life requiring a special essence, and today's skeptics who insist machine intelligence is impossible are making the same mistake. Profound things, including intelligence, are built out of mundane parts.
