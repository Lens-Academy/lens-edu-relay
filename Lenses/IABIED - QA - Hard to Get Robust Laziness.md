#### Text
content::
Making AIs "lazy" — too unmotivated to take over the world — sounds appealing but faces deep obstacles. A lazy but smart AI might simply build a harder-working servant to do things for it. The authors argue that "push on reality in this direction" is a simpler and more stable internal structure than "push a little but not too much," and that laziness trained via gradient descent would likely be a surface patch that breaks under superintelligent load.
