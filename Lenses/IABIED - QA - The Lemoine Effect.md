#### Text
content::
The authors coin "the Lemoine effect": all alarms about AI are first raised too early by the most easily alarmed person, then correctly dismissed, then can't be raised again because society is now inoculated against that concern. AIs have already tried to deceive, escape, and self-modify -- but in small, unimpressive ways that make researchers complacent. There may never be a bright red line, only a gradually reddening shade of brown.
