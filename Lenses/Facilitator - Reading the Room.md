#### Text
content::
Most of what a participant is communicating isn't in their words. Online, the signals are subtler but still legible.

\## In-person signals to watch for

- Posture change (leaning in / pulling back)
- Eye contact (with you, with each other, with the floor)
- Silence after a question (5 seconds of silence in the right context is gold; in the wrong context is fear)

\## Online signals to watch for

- **Mute status changes** — someone unmuted and then muted: they wanted to say something and pulled back; that's worth calling on
- **Camera state changes** — camera off mid-session = something happened
- **Typing in chat** — someone is writing something they're not sure about saying
- **Background noise / off-camera attention** — kids, other tabs
- **Face changes** — even at low resolution, you can see disengagement vs. thinking

\## The corresponding move: name what you see

A skill that pairs with reading the room is being willing to *name* what you're noticing — gently. Examples:

- *"I notice we got quiet after Maya's point. What's that about?"*
- *"I'm seeing several people nodding — anyone want to add to that?"*
- *"Tariq, you unmuted and then didn't say it. I'd be curious."*

Naming what's in the room takes practice — it can feel intrusive at first. But it often moves the room more than another general *"anyone else?"*
