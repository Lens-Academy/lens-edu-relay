#### Text
content::
Here's how to prep efficiently for a session you're about to facilitate.

\## Prep that actually moves session quality (target: 20–30 minutes)

1. **Read the reading yourself, once, with the participant's eye.** What was confusing? What was unexpected? What did you want to argue with? These are the spots the session will probably land on — knowing them in advance means you're not surprised.
2. **Walk through the meeting template once.** Read the questions, the activities, the timing. Note where you're least confident — the discussion question you don't quite see the point of, the activity you've never run before. Bring those to the facilitator channel before the session if needed.
3. **A backup plan for one foreseeable problem.** Usually one of: nobody did the reading; one person dominates; the topic is touchier than expected. Pick the most likely and have a first move ready.
4. **Personalise the template's check-in or framing in your own voice.** The template gives you the structure; small touches in your own voice make it land. Don't rewrite the template — just don't read it robotically.

\## Prep that's busywork

- Scripting your opening word-for-word.
- Re-reading the reading three times.
- Writing out detailed answers to the template's discussion questions. (You shouldn't be answering them.)
- Designing your own activities to replace what the template has. If something in the template doesn't work, flag it to the team — don't quietly replace it.
