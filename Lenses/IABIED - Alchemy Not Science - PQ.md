#### Text
content:: Imagine someone who can reliably produce a result — say, a sourdough starter that always rises, a chemical reaction that always yields the right precipitate, a stretch routine that consistently helps their back — *but who cannot explain why their procedure works.*

In what kind of situation does that recipe-level competence stop being enough? When does the gap between *knowing what works* and *knowing why it works* matter?

Take 60 seconds and write down what you think distinguishes "I have a recipe" from "I understand the principle" — and a situation where having only the recipe could become dangerous.
