\## In-person signals to watch for

- Posture change (leaning in / pulling back)
- Eye contact (with you, with each other, with the floor)
- Silence after a question (5 seconds {++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@of silence ++}in the right context is gold; in the wrong context is fear)

\## Online signals to watch for

- {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@**Mute-status --}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@**Mute status ++}changes** — someone unmuted and then{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@ muted =--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@ muted:++} they wanted to say something and pulled back; {++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@that's ++}worth calling on
- **Camera state changes** — camera off mid-session = something happened
- **Typing in chat** — someone is writing something they're not sure about saying
- **Background noise / off-camera attention** — kids, other tabs
- **Face changes** — even at low resolution, you can see disengagement vs. thinking

\## ++}The corresponding move: name what you see

{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@The--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@A++} skill that pairs with reading the room is being willing to *name* what you're noticing — gently. Examples:

- *"I notice we got quiet after Maya's point. What's that about?"*
- *"I'm seeing several people nodding — anyone want to add to that?"*
- *"Tariq, you unmuted and then didn't say it. I'd be curious."*

Naming what's in the room takes practice — it can feel intrusive at first. But it {--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@almost always--}{++{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@often++} moves the room more than another general *"anyone else?"*{--{"author":"Yatharth's AI","timestamp":1781125458909}@@

\## After

Pick the three signals you're most likely to actually notice in your sessions. For each, write the *name-what-you-see* move you'd use.

instructions::
The trainee has just watched the Reading the Room video.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Name three online non-verbal signals and craft two specific name-what-you-see moves.

Key concepts:
- Online signals: mute changes, camera changes, typing in chat, background distraction, face changes
- Pair signals with the name-what-you-see move
- Naming feels intrusive at first; gets easier with practice
- Signal-reading must be paired with action — passive observation does nothing

Discussion guidance:
- If their name-what-you-see move sounds aggressive, soften the framing. "I noticed X" is gentler than "Why did you X?"
- If they're uncertain about misreading a signal, walk through it: name tentatively, leave space for correction. "I might be misreading — is that what's happening?"

Probe: "What's the worst case if you name what you see and you're wrong? Is it actually worse than not naming it?"--}
