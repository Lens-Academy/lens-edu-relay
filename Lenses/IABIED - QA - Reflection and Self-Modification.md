#### Text
content::
This article argues that self-reflection and self-modification won't save us from misaligned AI. When agents reflect on their goals, they use their *current* preferences to guide changes -- just as humans don't self-modify toward maximizing gene propagation (evolution's "goal") but toward what we already value. An AI with alien initial preferences would self-modify in alien directions, potentially stripping out seeds of curiosity or kindness once it's smart enough to replace them with explicit calculation. The authors also explore how human goal-change is far messier than decision theory predicts, with instrumental values becoming terminal and formative experiences introducing deep contingency -- implying that AI goal development will be similarly unpredictable but in its own unique ways.
