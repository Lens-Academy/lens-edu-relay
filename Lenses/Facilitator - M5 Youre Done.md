content:: You now have the conceptual baseline of facilitation. The rest is reps.

A few last things worth knowing:

- **Your first session will not go like you planned.** That's normal. Run it anyway; debrief honestly with yourself afterward.
- **Keep a tiny journal.** After each session: one thing that worked, one thing to try differently next time. Five minutes. Over a course, this is worth more than re-reading this guide.
- **Talk to other facilitators.** Compare notes. Most of the best facilitation moves are learned from other facilitators describing what they tried.
- **Be kind to yourself.** This is a craft. You will be bad at it before you are good at it. So is everyone.

Good luck.
