#### Text
content::
\## Read

**Read: How Effective Is Think-Pair-Share? — Durrington Research School (3 min):** https://researchschool.org.uk/durrington/news/how-effective-is-think-pair-share

Think-Pair-Share is a three-step structure:

1. **Think (1-3 min):** pose a question. Everyone thinks silently or writes a quick note. No talking yet.
2. **Pair (3-5 min):** people break into pairs (or triads) and discuss their answer with one other person.
3. **Share (5-10 min):** pairs report back to the whole group.

\## Why it works

- The **think** phase makes use of the generation effect — people generate their own answer before being influenced by others.
- The **pair** phase gives every single participant speaking time — not just the two extroverts.
- The **share** phase produces higher-quality contributions, because people have already rehearsed their thinking with one other human.

\## When to use it

- You've asked a question and the group has gone quiet.
- The first share dominated, and you want to break the gravitational pull of that answer.
- The topic is sensitive and people need a smaller space to test their thinking before going public.
- You want the quiet members to participate without putting them on the spot.

In an online setting, "pair" maps to a 4-5 minute breakout room (2-3 people). This is one of the few moments where Zoom's breakout-room feature genuinely earns its weight. Don't waste it on trivial pairings.

\## After

Imagine you're at the top of a session. The reading was a 20-page chapter on a topic. You have 45 minutes. Write down (just for yourself):

1. **One Think-Pair-Share question** you could open with. *(Not "what did you think of the chapter?" — too vague. Something specific enough that a participant can have a defensible take.)*
2. **One mechanism from §2.1** that your opening question is leveraging. (Generation effect? Elaboration? Both?)
3. **One thing you would NOT do** at the top of the session, even though you'll want to.

If you have an actual upcoming session, do this for that one. The first 5 minutes of every session is the highest-leverage time. Plan it.

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just read the Durrington piece on Think-Pair-Share.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Describe the three steps of Think-Pair-Share, identify two situations where it's the right move, and write a Think-Pair-Share question for their own next session.

Key concepts:
- Three steps: Think (silent, 1-3 min) → Pair (3-5 min) → Share (5-10 min)
- Each phase leverages a different mechanism: generation effect, speaking time for all, rehearsed contributions
- Online: "Pair" = breakout rooms of 2-3 for 4-5 min — don't waste this on trivial pairings

Discussion guidance:
- If they propose a vague question ("what did you think?") — reject. Make them sharpen until the question has a defensible take.
- If they want to skip the silent think phase ("people already had time before the session") — push back. The think phase is doing the generation work.
- The 4-5 min breakout duration matters; less than that, people don't really engage; more, and it drags.

Probe: "What's a question you would NOT want to use Think-Pair-Share on — where it would be the wrong tool? Why?"
