#### Text
content::
This article argues that gradient descent and natural selection are fundamentally different optimizers, and that this difference matters enormously for what kind of minds they produce. Natural selection's narrow information bottleneck (a small genome) forced it toward simple hacks like genuine friendship, while gradient descent can encode vastly more patterns and has no such constraint -- meaning we should not expect it to converge on human-like values even in human-like training environments. The authors also speculate that current LLMs are "shallow" compared to humans precisely because they consist of enormous collections of relatively shallow memorized policy-patterns rather than deep cognitive structures.
