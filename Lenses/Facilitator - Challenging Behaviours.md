#### Text
content::
Some hard moments are about specific behaviours rather than your own slips. The most common:

| Behaviour | What you might try |
| :---- | :---- |
| **The dominator** — talks 3× more than anyone else | *"Maya, let me hold that for a moment. I want to hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet."* + private message after: *"I love your engagement; can we be partners in creating space for the quieter folks?"* |
| **The cynic** — every idea gets shot down | Don't argue. Get curious: *"You're sceptical. What's the version of this you'd actually find compelling?"* |
| **The off-topic-er** — keeps pulling threads away | Bookmark explicitly: *"That's interesting; let me hold it. We can come back if there's time. Returning to the question — [refocus]."* |
| **The personal sharer** — bringing too-much emotional content | Acknowledge, gentle redirect: *"Thank you for sharing that. Let's hold that with care, and let me bring us back to the material — though I'm happy to follow up after."* |
| **The grandstander** — makes long impressive statements that aren't actually engaging the discussion | Reflect briefly, redirect: *"So you're saying X — Tariq, how does that land for you?"* — give them less air than they want, transfer it. |

\## The general principle

Challenging behaviours are almost never about *you*. They're about what the participant is bringing in — their stake in the topic, their relationship to authority, what kind of day they're having. Don't internalise them; don't escalate against them; gently redirect, and follow up privately when needed.
