content:: **Closing exercise (4 min)**

Imagine you're at the top of a session. The reading was a 20-page chapter on a topic. You have 45 minutes.

Write down (just for yourself):

1. **One Think-Pair-Share question** you could open with. *(Not "what did you think of the chapter?" — too vague. Something specific enough that a participant can have a defensible take.)*
2. **One mechanism from §2.1** that your opening question is leveraging. (Generation effect? Elaboration? Both?)
3. **One thing you would NOT do** at the top of the session, even though you'll want to.

You don't have to share this with anyone. Writing it is the point. If you can do this for one of your actual upcoming sessions, do it for that one — the first 5 minutes of every session is the highest-leverage time. Plan it.
