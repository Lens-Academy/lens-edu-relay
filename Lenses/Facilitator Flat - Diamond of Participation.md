#### Text
content::
Lens sessions run 1.5–2 hours and are made up of several shorter components — discussions, activities, breakouts, reflections — not one continuous arc. The session as a whole doesn't follow a single shape.

But individual discussion components within a session usually do. The shape, when you see it, has three phases:

- **Open.** Surface what people are bringing. *Narrow.*
- **Explore.** Multiple perspectives, questions, working with the material together. *Wide.*
- **Close.** Re-narrow. What stuck? What's the takeaway? *Narrow again.*

This is sometimes called the **Diamond of Participation**. The name isn't important; the recognition is — because when participants try to skip ahead within a discussion (*"OK so the takeaway is…"*) or stall in one phase, your job is to bring them back to where the template is.

You don't need to design this — the template does. You just need to recognise it.
