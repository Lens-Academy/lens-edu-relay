#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: Some hard moments are about specific behaviours, not your slips. The five most common: the dominator (3× anyone else), the cynic (every idea gets shot down), the off-topic-er (keeps pulling threads away), the personal sharer (too much emotional content), the grandstander (long impressive statements that don't engage).

{--{"author":"AI","timestamp":1781085678824}@@**Pick--}{++{"author":"AI","timestamp":1781085678824}@@Pick++} four of those five. For each, name the specific facilitator move you'd use. Then articulate the general principle behind handling all of {--{"author":"AI","timestamp":1781085678824}@@them.**--}{++{"author":"AI","timestamp":1781085678824}@@them.++}

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Treats challenging behaviour as a personal attack. Defaults to confrontation or avoidance.

**2** — Names some moves but treats each behaviour as a unique problem requiring a unique solution.

**3** — Picks four of five with specific moves (dominator: hold + private message later; cynic: get curious about their compelling version; off-topic-er: bookmark; personal sharer: acknowledge + gentle redirect + private follow-up; grandstander: reflect briefly + transfer to next person). Articulates the general principle: challenging behaviours are almost never about *you*.

**4** — As above, plus articulates that the behaviours are about what the participant is bringing in (their stake in the topic, their relationship to authority, their day).

**5** — As above, plus identifies which behaviour is the one they'd find hardest to address in real time, and pre-commits to a specific opening sentence they'd practice.
