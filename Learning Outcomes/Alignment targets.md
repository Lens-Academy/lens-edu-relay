#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: An "alignment target" is the vision of how an AI should behave that we are trying to align it with. Pick two of the alignment targets discussed in this module (for example: coherent extrapolated volition, intent alignment, alignment to a constitution, or corrigibility), explain what each one means, and describe one way they differ from each other.
assessment-instructions:: A strong answer correctly explains two of these alignment targets and contrasts them. Reference points from the module: (1) Coherent extrapolated volition (CEV) = aligning AI to what we would want "if we knew more, thought faster, were more the people we wished we were, had grown up farther together"; an idealized/extrapolated notion. (2) Intent alignment = the AI trying to do what the human wants it to do; tied to the operator's actual current intentions. (3) Alignment to a constitution (e.g. Anthropic's Claude) = following the prescriptions in a written constitution that balances user wishes, developer guidelines, general ethics, and oversight, rather than any single human's judgment. (4) Corrigibility = the AI lets us modify its goals/functions and even shut it down, so we can correct mistakes and iterate to lower risk. A good contrast example: intent alignment targets one operator's current wishes, whereas CEV targets idealized/extrapolated human values; or corrigibility is about accepting correction rather than about which values to hold. Award 4-5 for two correct explanations plus a genuine difference; 3 for two roughly-correct explanations with a weak/missing contrast; 1-2 if a target is misdescribed or only one is addressed. Accept paraphrase; do not require exact quotes.
