#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: AI safety covers several distinct risks, not just one. In your own words, name and briefly distinguish at least three of the major categories of AI risk introduced in this module, and give one concrete example of harm for one of them.
assessment-instructions:: A strong answer identifies at least three distinct risk categories from the module's risk landscape: (1) autonomous misaligned AI (an AI that pursues goals not in line with human wishes), (2) AI misuse for destruction (e.g. a person using AI to assist bioterrorism or other catastrophic harm), and (3) AI misuse for seizing power / concentration of power (e.g. enabling global totalitarianism). Credit also for naming "others" such as gradual disempowerment (humans losing control incrementally even if alignment is technically solved). The answer must keep the categories distinct — a key distinction is misalignment (the AI's own goals diverge from human wishes) vs. misuse (a human deliberately directs an aligned-to-them AI toward harm). Award a high score (4-5) for three clearly distinguished categories plus a concrete example; mid (3) for three categories with blurred distinctions or no example; low (1-2) if fewer than two categories or if misalignment and misuse are conflated. Do not require the learner to use the exact source terminology.
