content:: The last five minutes of a session matter disproportionately. People remember beginnings and endings; middles fade. A weak close costs you most of what the session built.

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Treats the close as "we're out of time, thanks." Cannot draft a ritual.

**2** — Names some elements of a close but cannot articulate the three-part structure.

**3** — Describes Reflect (one-sentence takeaway per person), Integrate (one action between now and next session), Ritual (small consistent move). Drafts a real ritual (a shared phrase, a closing breath, a single-word check-out — doesn't have to be elaborate; has to be consistent).

**4** — As above, plus names two cohort-building moves: use names + 1 fact about each person early/often; connect participants to each other ("Tariq, what you're saying sounds like what Maya raised in Week 2 — have you two talked?"); off-session connection (Slack, shared doc, between-session check-in).

**5** — As above, plus articulates that for a multi-session cohort, the final session needs more weight: reflect across the arc (not just the last session), name the cohort specifically, set up the after-life.
