## Test:
id:: 8ca12fea-78f2-40a2-b6b7-4ba3de557968

#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: Quiet doesn't mean disengaged. Sometimes the quiet person is the deepest thinker in the room; sometimes they're checked out. Your job is to find out, without putting them on the spot.

**Name three moves that engage quiet participants without cold-calling. Then name the two patterns to AVOID (and explain the cost of each).**

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Defaults to cold-calling. Doesn't recognise it as costly.

**2** — Names some moves but cannot distinguish them from cold-calling.

**3** — Names three (pair them up via Think-Pair-Share; direct invitation with explicit out; reference their non-verbal; ask for specific kind of input; wait longer than comfortable). Names two avoid-patterns: cold-calling without warning; always calling on easy talkers.

**4** — As above, plus articulates why cold-calling costs: many freeze; the cost of freezing is high — they'll be quieter for weeks after.

**5** — As above, plus identifies that ranked praise ("great point, Maya!") makes the next person worry their point won't be "great" enough — use neutral acknowledgment.
