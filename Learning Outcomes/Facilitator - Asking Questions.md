## Test:
id:: fa91d02a-f35c-4df8-8da7-7e226f538bdf

#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: Most facilitators ask weak questions by default ("any thoughts?", "what did you think?") and get weak answers in return. Three frameworks improve this dramatically: **PPPB** (Pose, Pause, Pounce, Bounce), **ABC** (Agree, Build, Challenge), and **EEAA** (Engage, Evaluate, Apply, Adapt).

**Describe each framework in one sentence. Take one of your prepared questions for an upcoming session and walk through the PPPB version (how you pose it, the pause length, who you pounce on first). Then name which EEAA move you'd use as a follow-up if you got a substantive answer.**

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Cannot recall the frameworks. Defaults to "any thoughts?" without recognising it's weak.

**2** — Names the frameworks but cannot apply them concretely.

**3** — Describes all three. Walks through a real PPPB application of a real question (pose to whole group; pause silently ~7 seconds; pounce on a specific person; bounce their answer to someone else). Names a specific EEAA follow-up.

**4** — As above, plus articulates that rookies default to "Engage" for everything in EEAA — variety matters; "Evaluate" and "Adapt" are the under-used ones.

**5** — As above, plus pre-commits to memorising 2-3 "workhorse" question patterns (pre-mortem, translate, disagreement, instance, threshold) and identifies which one fits their next session best.
