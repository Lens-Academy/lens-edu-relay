assessment-instructions::
**1** — Can't identify a default. Treats both modes as equally likely.

**2** — Identifies a default but can't tie it to a real example or a recovery move.

**3** — Honestly identifies pufferfish or hermit crab with a real personal example. Names one concrete recovery move (e.g., for pufferfish: take a breath and ask a question instead of stating; for hermit crab: gently name the dynamic you're noticing).

**4** — As above, plus identifies the *trigger* — what specifically made the situation push them into their default (a tone of voice, a topic, a personality).

**5** — As above, plus pre-commits to a self-check cue (something they'll notice in their own body or behaviour in the first 10 seconds of the failure mode kicking in).
