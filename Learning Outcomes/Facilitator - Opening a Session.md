## Test:
id:: bc8eddaa-2259-4c7e-b4b8-2030030bebaf

#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: The first 5-10 minutes set the rest. Three things to accomplish: land in the room, reconnect to the material, set a clear shape. A reliable 3-step pattern: Check-in (2-3 min) → Anchor (1-2 min) → Opening question via Think-Pair-Share (3-5 min).

**Describe each of the three steps. Then draft your own opening plan for an upcoming session: check-in question, anchor sentence, and opening question.**

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Treats the open as small-talk before the "real" content. Cannot draft a real opening.

**2** — Names the three steps but cannot give concrete examples.

**3** — Correctly describes Check-in (low-stakes one-sentence per person), Anchor (1-2 line restatement of what THIS session is about — not "today we cover chapters 4-6"), Opening Question (specific enough to have a defensible take). Drafts a concrete opening plan for their own session.

**4** — As above, plus articulates the cohort-online moves: cameras on by norm, names + 1 fact on screen, chat used deliberately not as background noise.

**5** — As above, plus pre-commits to the timekeeping discipline (gently moving someone on if their check-in goes long — sets the norm that check-ins are short).
