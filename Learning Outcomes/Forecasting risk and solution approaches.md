## Test:
id:: 846bc26c-5dfe-4370-a4ff-e6ef62b0a127
#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: There is a broad spectrum of views on how hard AI alignment is and how to approach it. (a) Describe the range of views on the *difficulty* of the problem, using the three scenarios from Anthropic's "Core Views on AI Safety" as your scaffold. (b) Describe at least two different high-level *solution approaches* the field pursues, and explain how they differ.
assessment-instructions:: A strong answer covers both parts. (a) The three scenarios from Anthropic's "Core Views": an optimistic scenario (current methods are essentially sufficient); an intermediate scenario (preventing catastrophic risks requires substantial scientific and engineering effort); and a pessimistic scenario (AI safety is essentially unsolvable). Credit for noting the wider spread of views beyond Anthropic's framing — e.g. failure stories like "What failure looks like" or AI 2027 on the pessimistic side, "AI is easy to control" on the optimistic side, and that 38-51% of surveyed top-ML-venue authors gave at least 10% chance of extinction-level outcomes — and that under this uncertainty, research on the level/nature of risk itself (e.g. model organisms of misalignment) is useful. (b) At least two of these high-level solution approaches, correctly contrasted: empirical/iterative work (e.g. Jan Leike — iterate empirically, possibly using more advanced AI to help solve remaining problems); deep mathematical work (e.g. Yudkowsky's "rocket alignment" analogy — mathematical understanding before building); philosophical/conceptual work (solving deep philosophical problems en route to aligned AI); and prosaic AI alignment (Christiano — align the AI systems we already have rather than waiting for new paradigms; orthogonal to the empirical/theoretical split). Credit for noting the module's own stated synthesis: assume the prosaic deep-learning picture, while pursuing deep, empirically grounded *mathematical* progress. Award 4-5 for both parts with a real contrast; 3 for one part solid; 1-2 if the difficulty spectrum is collapsed to a single view or only one solution approach is named. Accept paraphrase; do not require exact author attributions.
