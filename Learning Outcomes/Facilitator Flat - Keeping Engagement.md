## Test:
id:: 18f9016f-accb-4084-a7ac-8088c1aab831

#### Question
feedback:: true
content:: Long sessions decay. The energy you have at minute 10 is not the energy you have at minute 40. Four practical antidotes work reliably — and one of them tends to produce the best contributions of the whole session.

**Name four antidotes to mid-session decay. For the "surface what hasn't been said" move — why does it often produce the strongest contributions?**

assessment-instructions::
**1** — Treats energy decay as the cohort's problem, not the facilitator's. Cannot name antidotes.

**2** — Names some antidotes but cannot articulate why "what hasn't been said" works.

**3** — Names all four: vary the format (whole-group → pair → individual reflection → whole-group); signpost what's next (let people know the shape); use specific names ("Maya raised X; Tariq pushed back") instead of "many of you mentioned"; surface what hasn't been said. Explains the last one works because it explicitly invites the contributions the social dynamic suppressed.

**4** — As above, plus articulates that signposting works because people engage harder when they know the shape — and that "many of you mentioned X" is dead air compared to specific names.

**5** — As above, plus articulates the format-variation rule of thumb: don't stay in any one mode for more than ~15 minutes. The shift itself resets attention.
