\## Read

**Read: Challenging Behaviours in Your Facilitation Flow… and What to Do About Them — Linn Vizard (10 min):** https://linnvizard.medium.com/challenging-behaviours-in-your-facilitation-flow-and-what-to-do-about-them-4b8846a46b9e

\## Five common patterns and moves

| Behaviour | What you might try |
| :---- | :---- |
| **The dominator** — talks 3× more than anyone else | *"Maya, let me hold that for a moment. I want to hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet."* + private message after: *"I love your engagement; can we be partners in creating space for the quieter folks?"* |
| **The cynic** — every idea gets shot down | Don't argue. Get curious: *"You're sceptical. What's the version of this you'd actually find compelling?"* |
| **The off-topic-er** — keeps pulling threads away | Bookmark explicitly: *"That's interesting; let me hold it. We can come back if there's time. Returning to the question — [refocus]."* |
| **The personal sharer** — bringing too-much emotional content | Acknowledge, gentle redirect: *"Thank you for sharing that. Let's hold that with care, and let me bring us back to the material — though I'm happy to follow up after."* |
| **The grandstander** — long impressive statements that aren't engaging the discussion | Reflect briefly, redirect: *"So you're saying X — Tariq, how does that land for you?"* — give them less air than they want, transfer it. |

\## The general principle

Challenging behaviours are almost never about *you*. They're about what the participant is bringing in — their stake in the topic, their relationship to authority, what kind of day they're having. Don't internalise them; don't escalate against them; gently redirect, and follow up privately when needed.

\## After

Pick the one behaviour you'd find hardest to address in real time. Write the specific opening sentence you'd practice.

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just read Vizard's piece.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Identify specific facilitator moves for four challenging behaviours, and articulate the general principle.

Key concepts:
- Five patterns: dominator, cynic, off-topic-er, personal sharer, grandstander
- Each has a specific move; range matters
- The general principle: it's almost never about you
- Private follow-up after the session is part of the move, especially for the dominator

Discussion guidance:
- If they want to confront the cynic, push: arguing rarely works. Curiosity does. The cynic often has a real concern buried in the dismissals.
- The personal sharer is the hardest for many — they don't want to seem cold. Walk through the *acknowledge + redirect + private follow-up* sequence.
- If they want to ignore the grandstander hoping they'll stop — they won't. The transfer move is the way.

Probe: "What's the situation in which 'gently redirect' isn't enough — and what would you escalate to?"
