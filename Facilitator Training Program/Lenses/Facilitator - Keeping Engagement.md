\## Four antidotes

- **Vary the format.** Whole-group → pair → whole-group → individual reflection → whole-group. The shifts reset attention.
- **Signpost what's next.** *"We'll spend 10 minutes on this, then move to the second question."* People engage harder when they know the shape.
- **Use specific names.** *"Many of you mentioned X"* is dead air. *"Maya raised X; Tariq pushed back; Aanya took a third angle"* is alive.
- **Surface what hasn't been said.** Halfway through, ask: *"What hasn't anyone said yet that should be said?"* — this often produces the best contributions of the session.

\## After

For your next session, plan one format shift at the halfway mark. What will you shift from, and to? And practice using participant names specifically when you summarise where the conversation has been.

#### Chat
instructions::
The trainee has just watched the Digges Keeping Engagement video.

Learning outcome for this Lens: Name four antidotes to energy decay, and articulate why surfacing "what hasn't been said" works.

Key concepts:
- Vary format (don't stay in one mode >15 min)
- Signpost what's next — engagement is partly about knowing the shape
- Specific names beat "many of you" or "some people"
- "What hasn't been said yet?" invites the suppressed contributions

Discussion guidance:
- If they think they don't need to signpost ("the agenda is on the screen"), push: signposting in the moment is different from agenda. It's about reorienting attention at transitions.
- If they're skeptical of "what hasn't been said yet," walk through why it works: the social dynamic of a session reliably suppresses certain contributions; explicitly inviting them lets them surface.

Probe: "If your energy is also dropping at minute 40, what move helps you reset — not just the group?"
